# Skill: Deal Pipeline Management

## Purpose
Track deals through stages from sourcing to close, ensuring nothing stalls or falls through the cracks.

## Pipeline Stages

| Stage | Duration | Key Activities | Exit Criteria |
|-------|----------|---------------|--------------|
| Sourced | — | Initial identification | Worth a closer look |
| Screening | 3-5 days | Quick financial check, market fit | Meets investment criteria |
| Analysis | 1-2 weeks | Comps, financial review, site research | Analyst + Underwriter reports complete |
| LOI / Offer | 3-5 days | Submit offer, negotiate terms | LOI accepted |
| Due Diligence | 30-60 days | Document review, inspections, financing | All items cleared |
| Closing | 1-2 weeks | Final walkthrough, funding, title | Keys in hand |
| Passed | — | Documented reason for passing | — |

## Deal Summary Format

```
# Deal Summary — [Property Name]

## Overview
- Address:
- Type:
- Units/SF:
- Asking Price:
- Cap Rate (asking):
- Cap Rate (underwritten):

## Status: [Stage]
- Days in stage:
- Next action:
- Deadline:

## Team Assessment
- Analyst: [comp-based value estimate]
- Underwriter: [risk score + key findings]
- Coordinator: [document status — X/Y received]
- Deal Maker: [recommendation]

## Investment Thesis
[2-3 sentences on why this deal is worth pursuing — or not]
```

## Rules

1. Every deal gets a summary file, updated at each stage transition
2. Deals in due diligence get daily status checks
3. Passed deals include documented reasoning (for learning)
4. Pipeline report generated weekly with all active deals
5. Human is notified of any deadline within 7 days
